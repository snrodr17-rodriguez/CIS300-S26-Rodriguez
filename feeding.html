Wet Food vs. Dry Food

The great debate. Both wet and dry food have their pros and cons, and the best choice for your cat will depend on their individual needs and preferences. Wet food is generally more palatable and can help your cat stay hydrated, but it can be more expensive and has a shorter shelf life once opened. Dry food is more convenient, but it can be less appealing to some cats and may not provide as much moisture. Cats tend to have a pretty low thirst drive, so it's important to make sure they are getting enough water, especially if they are eating dry food. You can also try combination feeding (wet and dry) to give your cat the best of both worlds!

Understanding Ingredients

One thing I can't stress enough is the importance of protein. Cat's are what you call obligate carnivores, which means that they require a diet that is high in animal protein to thrive. When looking at a cat food label, the first ingredient should always be some form of meat (chicken, beef, fish, etc.). I will say, certain meats are better tha others. For example, chicken and turkey are generally considered to be better sources of protein than beef or certain fish. Beef tends to be very rich and be hard for cats to digest, and certain fish (like tuna) can be high in mercury. This does not mean you have to completely stay away from these ingredients, but if you plan on feeding them, it'd be best to rotate them out with other, more easily digestible proteins. Before you go onto the next sentence, I am not a vet, nor do I have a degree in feline nutrition so please do your own research outside of this site and consult with your vet before making any major changes to your cat's diet. This might get some mixed opinions, but I personally try to avoid feeding my cat foods that contain a lot of plant-based ingredients (like corn, wheat, soy, etc.) as these are not natural components of a cat's diet and can be harder for them to digest. Again, this is just my personal preference and there are many cats that do just fine on diets that include these ingredients. The most important thing is to find something that your cat enjoys and meets their nutritional needs. "Fed is best" is a common phrase in the pet community, and it really is true.


One ingredient I will say you want to look for is taurine. Taurine is an essential amino acid that cats cannot produce on their own, so it must be include in their diet. It's important for maintaining healthy vision, heart functins, and reproductive health. You can find taurine in animal-based ingredients like meat and fish, but it's also commonly added to commercial cat foods as a supplement.
