Maine Coon

Origin: Maine, United States

Temperament: Friendly, Playful, Intelligent

The Maine Coon is one of the largest domesticated cat breeds. They are ultimately one of the best-known cat breeds as well. They are a friendly, sweet-tempered and easy to train breed that is well-suited to fit the frosty winters of the northeast U.S. They have heavy, flowing coats for built-in protection from the cold and broad, well-tufted paws that appear designed to navigate snow banks (they are from Maine and all). Like most of the other breeds discussed on this website, they enjoy interacting with every family member and tend to go from on elap to another, rather than choosing a favorite. They are also quite fond of water and some might even watch a dripping faucet in fascination!


Despite the love they show to their humans, they are also an independent breed that need a moderate amount of human interaction and will insist upon it when you are available. They are good cats that can get along other children and other animals, but both could be inimidated by its larger size. They are larger than many small dogs! Physically, these cats have a thick coat that breeders recommend combing two or three times a week to protect against matting. It's a good idea to get your Maine Coon kitten familiar with the groomers so they can take good care of your kitty.
