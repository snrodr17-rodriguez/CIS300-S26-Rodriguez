Bengal

Origin: California, United States

Temperament: Very active, curious, and athletic.

Having a Bengal is like having a little wild cat in your home. They are extremely intelligent and maintain that kitten-like energy and attitude well into their senior years. These cats are fascinated by water and you might find them trying to explore your shower or sinks. If you choose to buy a Bengal, it's important to understand that these are cat's that require a lot of stimulation to prevent them from destroying your household. Due to their intelligence, they are quite trainable and often learn to do tricks. Many people even take them out with a harness and a leash! When properly introduced with other pets, they can get along well with said pets and be a great addition to the family.


Bengals are a beautiful breed and while there are plenty of other spotted breeds, Bengals are the only breed with true rosettes that have dark outlines. These cats descended from a wild ancestor however after many generations of careful breeding, they are domesticed yet dedicated to replicating the look of a wild cat. Selecting for the best temperaments has been a top priority for Bengal breeders. Today, a Bengal is a good companion that is as playful and loving as anyone could want.
