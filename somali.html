Somali

Origin: Shared Origin, Canada & United States

Temperament: Active, curious, and bold.

One of my personal favorites the Somali breed is a direct descendant of the Abyssinian. It is essentially a long-haired variety but remains it's own recognized breed since the 1960s. Like most cats, Somalis are a curious breed. If they are provided with plenty of toys, they are capable of amusing themselves for hours, but tend to be happiest when engaged with some sort of interactive play with another pet or human. They have a very sweet and well-balance temperament and will often demonstrated their affection for their owners with head butts and kneading.


These are a medium size cat breed and are well-proportioned, solid, and muscular with silky, medium-length coats! They're not a very challenging cat to groom as their coat is relatively tangle free and resists matting. However, it's still recommended to comb weekly to remove any dead hair and control shedding.
