American Shorthair

Origin: United States

Temperament: Affectionate, playful, and intelligent.

Sleek, rugged, and self-sufficient, the American Shorthair is frequently described as a "working cat." Although they prefer to leave household activities and work to you, they will do their best to supervise! Shorthaired cats arrived in North America with the early settlers and proved to be good pest control. As these pioneers moved westward, their cats earned their keep by continuing their pest control jobs. A breed was born! When the first cat show was held in the United States in 1895, over 50 were present! Originally, they were shown as "Domestic Shorthairs" but received their current name in 1965.


These cats are affectionate by nature, and they tend to appreciate human company as well as interactive play with their own families. However, they also can be quite independent, enough to amuse themselves while their owners are gone at work or doing other things. Due to their high intelligence, they tend to be highly trainable and can be taught to stay off counters and even retrieve toys.
